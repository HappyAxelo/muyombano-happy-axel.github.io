About

I am a Research Assistant at the Transformative Artificial Intelligence Research and Innovation Lab at the University of Rwanda, where I completed my MSc in Embedded Computer Systems with concentrations in Artificial Intelligence and Internet of Things.

I am passionate about technology-driven impact on social lives. With over 4 years of diverse experience through internships, volunteering, and engineering roles, I bring a combination of technical knowledge, project management, and business analysis skills. I also serve as Managing Director at Ampere Vision Rwanda Ltd, focusing on AI solutions and product development.

My work includes developing AI-powered solutions for social impact, such as "Shisha Mwana" - an AI mobile-based malnutrition early detection and prevention system in collaboration with NCST & RBC.

IEEE MTTS at CMU

The Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE) Microwave Theory and Technology Society (MTTS) event at Carnegie Mellon University Africa brought together students from this prestigious engineering institution and IEEE communities worldwide to learn, collaborate, and exchange innovative ideas in microwave theory and technology. As the Chairperson of the IEEE Student Branch at the University of Rwanda, I am proud to have been part of the organizing team that contributed to making this event a remarkable success. My sincere appreciation goes to the dedicated team led by Prof. Edwin Mugume for their exceptional leadership and for delivering such an impactful and inspiring event.

On 17th of October 2025, I celebrated a proud milestone in my journey as I graduated with my master's degree. My deepest gratitude goes to my mother for her unwavering support, love, and encouragement throughout every step. I am also sincerely thankful to the African Centre of Excellence in Internet of Things (ACEIoT) for awarding me the scholarship that made this achievement possible. Exhibition of our innovation

I had the honour of exhibiting our bird repeller technology in Uganda during the 1st Regional Ministerial Conference on the East African Common Higher Education Area (EACHEA). It was a privilege to showcase our innovative solutions to Ministers of Education from various East African countries and to contribute to regional conversations on technology and education. My sincere appreciation goes to the University of Rwanda leadership for their guidance and unwavering support throughout this journey.
